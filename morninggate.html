The Tic Tac Toe Experience - Gatekeeper

  • Start Reading
the story begins...

For as long as he could remember, David had been drawn to the ancient gate that stood at the edge of the forest near his small hometown. The gate was said to lead to a place of mystery and wonder, but it was also rumored to be haunted. Most townsfolk avoided it, considering it a relic of a forgotten era.

One moonless night, as David gazed at the gate from a distance, he heard a faint, eerie voice calling to him. At first, he dismissed it as his imagination playing tricks on him. But the voice grew stronger, more insistent, until it felt as though it was inside his head. "Come closer, David," the voice whispered, chilling his spine.

He cautiously approached the gate, wondering if he was dreaming or losing his sanity. The voice seemed to emanate from the darkness beyond the gate. "What do you want?" David asked, his voice quivering. "I want to play a game," the voice replied, now taking on an unsettling, disembodied laughter.

David hesitated, but his curiosity got the better of him. "What kind of game?" The voice explained the rules. It was a game of Tic Tac Toe, but with a sinister twist. David was to play against an unknown entity on the other side of the gate.

If he won, he could pass through, but if he lost, he would belong to the entity forever. With trembling hands, David agreed to the game. He drew the Tic Tac Toe grid on the ground, and on the other side, he heard a soft, ghostly hand making its move.

The game was intense, and David felt the weight of his very soul hanging in the balance. He managed to win, placing his final "X" in the winning spot. The gate creaked open, and he cautiously passed through.

As the first light of dawn broke, David felt relieved. He had won the game, and the mysterious entity had disappeared. The world on the other side of the gate appeared serene and untouched by time. But something had changed within him.

As days turned into weeks, those who knew David began to notice a shift in his personality. He became distant, aloof, and his once-friendly demeanor had turned cold and calculating. David himself felt like a passenger in his own body, observing his actions as if from a distance. Slowly, the truth began to emerge.

The entity from beyond the gate had not vanished; it had taken up residence within him. It had merged with his consciousness, and David was no longer in control of his own destiny. The once-curious young man was now a vessel for a malevolent force, trapped within his own body, while the entity played a deadly game of Tic Tac Toe with the souls of those who dared to pass through the gate.

And as David watched helplessly from within, he realized that he had become the gatekeeper, forever bound to the ancient portal, forced to lure others into the same nightmarish fate he now endured.
